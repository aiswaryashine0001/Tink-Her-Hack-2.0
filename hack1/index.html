What is Human Trafficking?

Human trafficking is a form of modern-day slavery involving the exploitation of individuals through force, fraud, or coercion for purposes such as forced labor, sexual exploitation, or commercial sexual exploitation. It deprives victims of their freedom and dignity, often involving the recruitment, transportation, transfer, harboring, or receipt of persons through the use of threat, force, fraud, or deception.

Signs of Human Trafficking

  • Physical signs of abuse or injury, such as bruises, cuts, or burns
  • Poor physical health and untreated medical conditions
  • Signs of psychological trauma, such as fear, anxiety, or depression
  • Unexplained absences from school, work, or social activities
  • Controlled or restricted communication, including limited access to a phone or inability to speak for oneself
  • Presence of a controlling or abusive person who seems to be in charge of the victim's actions
  • Inability to move or leave a job or living situation
  • Working excessively long hours or under hazardous conditions
  • Living in poor or overcrowded conditions
  • Lack of control over personal identification documents, such as passports or identification cards
  • Presence of multiple unrelated individuals living in the same residence, especially if they appear to be under the control of someone else

Report Human Trafficking

If you suspect someone is a victim of human trafficking, please report it immediately.
